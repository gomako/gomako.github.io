De-soldering2016-11-25

Finally found some time to desolder a few bits from the board, and amongst the bits I took off there was a nice little DC-DC step up convertor that took 3v from the batteries and stepped it up to 5v.

Next will be removing some more stuff to get things lying flat, do the low battery shutdown as explained here https://github.com/NeonHorizon/lipopi and getting the button traces cut up so I can solder them to the GPIO. I want my screen to arrive!

Start2016-11-22

Started this log. So far I have Retropie on a 16Gb SD card with a few ROMS to test, and am wanting to put it into a GB Pocket that has been knocking about in my family for about 20-something years. It doesn't work anymore so I'm going to gut it and fill it with a Pi Zero.

I am aiming for the following features:

  • Expose HDMI port for plugging into TV
  • Expose USB charging port (maybe using and adapter to utilize the link cable as the charging port)
  • Bluetooth to connect when unit is plugged into TV for multiplayer

The hardest part seems to be deciding on a screen. I don't want to use a reversing camera type one (even though that's the easiest route as it adds bulk and they are too big to fit into a GBP, so I'm waiting for the boat to arrive from China with my SPI screen in. I hope it's not too slow a refresh rate etc.
